Toucan

Description

Toucans are relatively small birds with exceptionally large beaks. Their long beaks are usually brightly colored, and much longer and thicker than their actual heads.


Interesting Facts

There are many different and beautiful species of Toucans, Toucanets, and Aracaris. Some interesting facts about these are listed below.

  • Toco Toucan : Toco is the largest species of Toucan in the world. They are also the most well-known of this species of bird. It has the largest bill (but not the longest) of all birds, in relation to its body size.
  • Choco Toucan : Choco is similar, but less colorful, than its Toco cousin. The name “Choco” is in reference to the forests that these birds inhabit. Choco Toucans live in the Choco rainforests.
  • Black-Necked Aracari : This species has black feathers and a bright yellow underbelly. As its name suggests, its throat is black. Midway down its yellow belly, it has a thick stripe of bright red feathers. These starkly contrasting colors are probably where this bird got its nickname, the beautiful Aracari.
  • Yellow-Browed Toucanet : This little bird has green feathers and bright yellow “eyebrows” over its eyes. They live only in a tiny patch of rainforest in Peru.
